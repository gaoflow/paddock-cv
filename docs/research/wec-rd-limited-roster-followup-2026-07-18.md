## Scope

Mans car page, then searched exact names and role variants through MySearch/Exa
and public web sources. Only public static portraits were eligible. No video,
video frame, reel, or video thumbnail was searched or used.

## Javier identity resolution

The initial search result appeared as an Instagram static post summary, but it
was not needed as evidence. A same-period local-news profile provides a
stronger identity chain:

1. Its title, caption and body name Javier Sánchez Vizcaíno.
2. It states that he worked as a track engineer with French team RD Limited.
3. It identifies the LMP2 ORECA as car #48 at the 2026 24 Hours of Le Mans.
4. It describes the systems and performance work represented by the car page's
   shorter `Data acquisition` label.

The full name therefore replaces the abbreviated unaccented event-page form;
no duplicate `Javier Sanchez` record was created.

## Avatar decisions

The Javier profile supplies a 1280 x 787 static image captioned with his full
name. It is a clear single-person pit-lane photograph. The image was downloaded,
decoded, visually inspected, cropped around the named subject and stored at
`web/img/javier-sanchez-vizcaino-andalucia-informacion.jpg` so the face remains
legible in both the roster avatar and centered detail modal.

No Santiago portrait was adopted. Exact-name image, LinkedIn, Instagram and
Facebook searches either returned no result or unrelated namesakes, so an empty
photo is more accurate than an ambiguous face.

## Result

RD Limited rises from four to six source members. Both missing #48 duties are
now represented once, Javier adds one verified local static portrait, and no
unsupported team-level employees or car assignments were introduced.
